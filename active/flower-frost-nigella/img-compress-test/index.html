Image compression test

I'm taking two images and compressing them until they are as small as possible, but 'not bad' to my eyes. So, lots of subjectivity, and not a lot of science. However, it gives a feel for the kind of quality I'd usually aim for.

I'm copying the size and density from The Guardian article pages.

All of the images were created in Squoosh.app.

City

Uncompressed

MozJPEG - 57kB

Quality 44. Banding in the sky prevented me going lower quality.

WebP - 40kB

Quality 24.9. Effort 6.

AVIF - 35kB

Quality 24, effort 10.

JXL - 39.3kB

Quality 15, progressive rendering, effort 3 (higher efforts seemed to increase size without improving quality).

Face

Uncompressed

MozJPEG - 60kB

Quality 50. Anything lower introduced severe blocks to the left-hand side of the face.

WebP - 36kB

Quality 20.5. Effort 6.

AVIF - 38Kb

Quality 22, effort 10. Any lower and the smoothing was just too much.

AVIF - 29kB

Quality 22, effort 10, full noise synthesis. This image looks good to me, but it's missing potentially significant details (like the mole on the face). Also I'm told the noise synthesis isn't well optimised yet.

JXL - 35.6kB

Quality 14.9, effort 3, progressive. This feels higher quality than the AVIF, but lower qualities introduced pretty noticable blockiness around the lips.
